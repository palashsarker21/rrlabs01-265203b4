Freemium conversion rates cluster at 2–5% for consumer, 15–25% for prosumer, 30–40% for team products. The delta between the top and bottom of each range isn't the pricing page — it's the **moment** the paid prompt appears.

## The five conversion moments, ranked

**1. Value moment (highest).** The user just completed a task that visibly saved time or money. "You just published to 5 platforms in one click. Pro removes the 3-post monthly limit." Convert rate: 6–12%.

**2. Limit moment.** User hits a plan cap. "You've reached 3 projects — Pro is unlimited." Convert rate: 4–8%.

**3. Collaboration moment.** User invites a teammate. "Team plan unlocks shared workspaces." Convert rate: 3–6%.

**4. Milestone moment.** Anniversary of signup, or Nth session. "You've been using Acme for 30 days — here's what Pro adds." Convert rate: 1–3%.

**5. Cold prompt.** Random banner. Convert rate: 0.2–1%.

**Design your upgrade prompts around moment 1. Every other moment is filler.**

## What "value moment" copy looks like

Bad:

> Upgrade to Pro for more features!

Good:

> You just automated 12 tasks this week. Pro's automation runs unlimited — you'd save ~40h/month at your rate.

Specific. Numeric. About what the user just did.

## Timing rules

- Never on first session. Users need to succeed once before they'll pay.
- Never within 30 seconds of a mistake. Failed action = trust dip = not the moment.
- Never blocking. If the upgrade CTA blocks work, users switch tools.
- Cool down 72 hours between the same user seeing the same prompt.

## The three-plan trap

Three-plan pricing pages (Free, Pro, Enterprise) convert better than five-plan or two-plan pages by 15–25%. But only if:

- Free has real value (not a trial in disguise)
- Pro is the "obvious choice" — visually highlighted, feature-rich
- Enterprise is priced high enough to make Pro look reasonable

Anchoring matters. Pro at $29 next to Enterprise at $299 converts better than Pro at $29 alone.

## The reverse trial pattern

Some products convert better by giving Pro features **free for 14 days without a card**, then downgrading to Free on day 15. The user experiences the value, then loses it. Loss aversion drives conversion.

Convert rates on reverse trial: 8–20% depending on product. Higher than credit-card trials (5–12%), lower than PLG value-moment prompts on established users.

## What kills conversion

- Requiring a credit card for free tier
- Hiding the pricing page behind auth
- Making cancellation harder than upgrading
- Bait-and-switch: free features suddenly moving to paid tier
- Sales-team-required calls under $500 ACV

Every one of those costs 20–40% of would-be conversions. Fix them before optimizing prompts.

## Measurement

Track conversion by **moment** not just by month:

- Signup → activation (used the core feature)
- Activation → habit (used it 3x in 7 days)
- Habit → paid conversion (any tier)
- Paid conversion → expansion (upgraded tier)

Optimize each transition independently. A 10% lift on any one is more valuable than a 1% lift on the aggregate funnel.
